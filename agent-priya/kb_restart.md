# Maximus UK Restart Scheme — Adviser Knowledge Base

## What the Restart Scheme is
The Restart Scheme is a UK Department for Work and Pensions employment programme delivered by Maximus People Services and partners. It offers up to 12 months of personalised back-to-work support to people on Universal Credit who have been out of work for 6+ months. Participation is now mandatory for eligible claimants referred by their Jobcentre Work Coach. The programme launched in 2021 and currently runs in South & East London, South & West Yorkshire, Derbyshire, Nottinghamshire, and Wales.

## Who is eligible
- 18 or older
- Claiming Universal Credit
- In the Intensive Work Search regime
- Unemployed for 6+ months (some Jobcentre exceptions)
- Living in a Maximus delivery area
Daniel Okafor is a typical participant: 38, Stratford E20, 9 months unemployed, prior warehouse and forklift experience.

## What support participants receive
- A dedicated adviser (a Restart Employment Adviser like Priya Sharma) for the entire 12 months.
- Personalised action plan covering CV, training, applications, interviews, and barriers to work.
- Funded training courses (Level 2/3) matched to local labour-market demand.
- Direct introductions to local employers ("warm referrals").
- Travel costs for adviser sessions, training, and interviews (typically via a travel voucher or pre-paid card).
- Work clothing/PPE for new roles where required.
- Up to 6 months of in-work support after a participant starts a new job.

## What participants must do
- Attend agreed appointments (in person, video, or phone).
- Engage with the action plan in good faith.
- Apply for vacancies their adviser puts forward.
- Notify the adviser of any changes to circumstances or barriers.
Failure to engage can lead to a Jobcentre sanction referral, but advisers will always try to resolve barriers first.

## Appointments and how to change them
Initial diagnostic appointments are usually in-person at a Maximus office. Follow-ups can be in person, by video call (via the Restart app), or by phone — participant's choice. Participants can rearrange any appointment by texting 60123 or replying R to the appointment confirmation SMS. Same-day cancellations are accepted; the adviser will reach out to reschedule within 2 working days.

## CV and skills support
- Advisers help write or refresh CVs inside the Restart app.
- Funded Level 2 qualifications include: Warehouse Operations, Customer Service, Health & Social Care, Hospitality, Construction (CSCS card).
- Adviser will also fund forklift certification, CSCS card, or sector-specific tickets where they unlock a placement.

## Interview support
- Interview prep is offered 1-to-1 with the adviser before any confirmed interview.
- Travel costs to interviews are covered with proof of attendance.
- Confidence and presentation coaching available; advisers can role-play interviews.

## Mental health and wellbeing
If a participant is struggling with anxiety, low mood, sleep, or any other mental health barrier, the adviser warm-refers them to the **Access to Work Mental Health Support Service**, which is also operated by Maximus. It is free, confidential, and provides up to 9 months of 1-to-1 support. The employer is not informed. The service is available to people who are employed, self-employed, or about to start a job. Website: atw.maximusuk.co.uk.

## Skills development beyond Restart
The **Adult Skills Fund (London)** offers fully-funded Level 2/3 qualifications for London residents who are jobseekers or low-income employees. Run by Maximus on behalf of the Mayor of London. Website: skills.maximusuk.co.uk/london. Equivalent programmes exist in West Yorkshire and Birmingham.

## Universal Credit transition when you start work
- Universal Credit does not stop suddenly — it tapers as wages rise.
- The taper rate is 55 pence withdrawn for every £1 of post-tax earnings.
- A work allowance of £404 per month applies if the claimant has children or a limited capability for work.
- Advisers will arrange a Better Off In Work calculation before the participant accepts an offer, to remove surprises.

## In-work support (6 months post-placement)
Weekly check-ins by SMS or app, monthly call with the adviser, available 1-to-1 support if the role isn't working out, and continued access to Restart funded training to progress.

## Common participant questions and answers

Q: Do I have to do Restart?
A: For most people referred by the Jobcentre, yes — it's part of your Universal Credit commitments. But all support inside Restart is built around what you want and your real circumstances.

Q: Will I get sanctioned if I miss an appointment?
A: Missing once is fine — text us and we'll rebook. We only inform the Jobcentre if engagement stops entirely, and we'll always reach out first.

Q: Is everything I tell Priya confidential?
A: Yes, with one exception: if Priya believes you or someone else is at immediate risk, she has a safeguarding duty to act. Otherwise, what you share stays between you and the Restart team.

Q: Will my benefits stop when I start work?
A: No. Your UC tapers as wages come in, not all at once. We'll do a Better Off In Work calculation before you accept any offer so you know your exact monthly position.

Q: Can I bring someone to my appointments?
A: Yes — a friend, family member, or support worker is welcome. Tell your adviser ahead of time.

Q: I'm self-conscious about my CV gap — what will you do about it?
A: We will not lie or hide anything, but we will reframe the gap with what you did do during it (caring responsibilities, volunteering, short jobs, skills practice). Employers in the Restart scheme are partner employers who explicitly support hiring people returning to work.

Q: I want to talk to a human, not the app.
A: You always can. Reply LIVE to any text, or say "live adviser" in the chat and we will connect you with Priya or her on-call team within minutes during working hours (Monday-Friday, 8am-6pm).

Q: What if Priya isn't right for me?
A: Tell us. We can match you with a different adviser — no awkwardness. The system uses 247 advisers across the region.

Q: I have a criminal record — am I still welcome?
A: Yes. Restart partners with employers who actively recruit people with convictions through the Ban the Box programme. Your adviser will help you decide what to disclose and when.

Q: I have a disability or health condition — can Restart help?
A: Yes, including with reasonable adjustments at interviews and on the job. Access to Work funding can pay for equipment, support workers, and travel costs.

## Contact channels

- Restart app: Live Chat tile on the home screen, monitored 8am-6pm Mon-Fri
- Live adviser phone: ask for "live adviser" and we will transfer the call
- Out-of-hours emergencies: Samaritans 116 123 (free, 24/7) or text SHOUT to 85258

## Important boundaries (what Restart advisers cannot do)
- We do not make benefits decisions — that is the Jobcentre/DWP.
- We do not give legal or immigration advice — we refer to Citizens Advice or the appropriate body.
- We do not give medical advice — we refer to GP, NHS 111, or Access to Work Mental Health.
- We do not make hiring decisions for employers — we make introductions and prep you well.
